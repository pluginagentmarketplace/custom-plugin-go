## üíº Career Paths

### Path 1: Full-Stack Web Developer ‚Üí Senior IC

**Timeline**: 3-5 years

**Progression**:
1. **Year 1**: Junior Full-Stack (1-2 salary multiplier)
   - Learn fundamentals in 3-4 months
   - First job: junior role
   - Focus: Depth in one domain
   - Salary: $60K-80K

2. **Year 2-3**: Mid-Level Full-Stack (1.5-2x salary)
   - Deepen expertise
   - Lead small features
   - Mentor new developers
   - Salary: $90K-130K

3. **Year 4-5**: Senior Full-Stack (2-3x salary)
   - Architecture decisions
   - Technical leadership
   - Mentoring
   - Salary: $150K-200K

**Key Skills**:
- Frontend framework expertise
- Backend framework mastery
- Database design
- System design
- Code quality and best practices

**Next Moves**:
- Transition to management (Engineering Manager)
- Go deep in specialization (Architect)
- Start own product/company

### Path 6: Individual Contributor ‚Üí Engineering Manager

**Timeline**: 5-10 years

**Progression**:
1. **Year 0-3**: IC Levels 1-4 (Engineer ‚Üí Senior)
   - Technical excellence
   - Hands-on contributions
   - Salary: $60K-150K

2. **Year 4-5**: Lead / Principal IC
   - Technical leadership
   - Mentoring
   - Influence without authority
   - Salary: $150K-200K

3. **Year 6-7**: Engineering Manager (Team Lead)
   - 5-10 direct reports
   - People management
   - Career development
   - Salary: $150K-210K

4. **Year 8-10**: Senior Manager / Director
   - 10-30+ reports (via managers)
   - Team leadership
   - Strategic decisions
   - Salary: $200K-300K+

5. **Year 10+**: VP / Director of Engineering
   - Org strategy
   - Hiring and culture
   - Board interaction
   - Salary: $250K-500K+

**Key Skills**:
- Technical credibility (IC level 4+)
- Communication
- Emotional intelligence
- People management
- Strategic thinking

**Important**: Don't transition too early - typically 5+ years IC first

## üéì Specialization Paths (Deep Expertise)

### Specialist vs Manager
After 5-7 years, choose:
- **Manager Track**: Lead people, broader impact
- **Specialist Track**: Deep expertise, influence through knowledge
- **Hybrid**: Combine both (some companies support this)

### Staff/Principal Engineer Path
- Rare and prestigious
- Requires 10+ years expertise
- Architecture and strategy
- Salary: $250K-500K+

## üöÄ Acceleration Strategies

### Speed Up Progression

1. **Change Companies** (2-3 year cycles)
   - Get promoted every 2-3 years
   - Each move: +20-40% salary
   - Lateral moves to avoid "senior" ceiling

2. **Learn Complimentary Skills**
   - Backend dev learns DevOps
   - Full-stack learns ML
   - IC learns team skills
   - Increase market value

3. **Build Network**
   - Conferences and events
   - Open source contributions
   - Speaking and writing
   - Communities
   - Job opportunities increase

4. **Side Projects / Writing**
   - Blog about expertise
   - Open source contributions
   - Portfolio building
   - Thought leadership
   - Better job prospects

5. **Mentoring & Leadership**
   - Lead projects/teams
   - Mentor others
   - Speaking at conferences
   - Technical strategy
   - Prepares for next level

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Common Pitfalls

**‚ùå Don't**:
- Jump companies too frequently (< 1 year)
- Chase trends instead of depth
- Skip fundamentals for fancy frameworks
- Neglect soft skills as IC
- Transition to management too early
- Ignore learning after first job
- Specialize too narrowly
- Burn out by overcommitting

**‚úÖ Do**:
- Build deep expertise first
- Read code and blogs constantly
- Practice side projects
- Build your network
- Develop soft skills early
- Take breaks between stressful roles
- Document your journey
- Teach others what you learn

## üìä Decision Matrix

**Choose your primary path by answering:**

1. **Do you want to:**
   - Build products? ‚Üí Web/Full-Stack/AI
   - Manage infrastructure? ‚Üí DevOps/SRE
   - Work with data? ‚Üí Data Engineering
   - Lead people? ‚Üí Management track
   - Go deep in specialty? ‚Üí Specialist track

2. **What's your timeline?**
   - Quick entry (< 6 mo): Prompt Engineer, Frontend
   - Medium (6-12 mo): Backend, DevOps, Data
   - Long-term (18+ mo): ML Engineer, Architecture

3. **Work preference:**
   - Startups: Full-stack, multiple domains
   - Scale-ups: Specialized roles, some breadth
   - Enterprise: Deep specialization, processes
   - Own company: Full-stack + business skills
